## 1. Types of Collectors & Their Goals

Collectors are not one-size-fits-all — their motivations drive how they spend, engage, and evolve across different collecting journeys.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Hobbyists** | Driven by fun, nostalgia, and sentimental value. Collecting is about enjoyment, community, and sharing — value appreciation is secondary. |
| **Investors** | Focused on long-term value and portfolio growth. Prioritize graded cards, rarity, and appreciation potential. |
| **Set Builders** | Motivated by completion and organization. Seek to finish full sets and enjoy the challenge of acquiring missing cards. |
| **Player / Team** | Dedicated to a favorite player, team, or character. Collect for passionate fandom, expertise, and personal connection. |
| **Flipper** | Opportunistic and transaction-driven. Buy and sell cards for profit with less emotional attachment. |
| **Novice** | New to the hobby and discovering what they enjoy. Collecting is about learning, experimentation, and first hits. |

## 2. How Collectors Build Their Collections

**Goal:** Inspire new collecting journeys through naming functions and incentive programs.

### A. Set Quests
Complete a defined checklist or card group.
- Complete set (e.g., 25 Topps BK base set)
- Master set (e.g., 25 Heritage master set)
- Insert set (e.g., Future Stars, Youthquake)
- Autograph set (e.g., Real One Autos)
- PSA set registry (e.g., complete set in PSA 10)
- Sticker set (e.g., sticker albums)

### B. Player / Team Quests
Build depth around a single player or character.
- Team sets (e.g., 1952 Topps base set NYY)
- Rainbow (1/1 and up)
- Player stack (same subject across card types)
- Player runs (same card across years)
- Jersey # match (serial # matches player)
- Prospects / Rookies chase (prospecting)
- PSA set registry (graded set — player or team)

### C. Attribute Quests
Collect across a shared theme, story, or rule.
- Milestone chase (MVPs, ROY, 500 HR Club)
- Nationality set/run (from their country)
- Unique relic chase (bat knobs)
- Parallel set/run (e.g., Gold /50, color match)
- Serial-number run (e.g., 07/99 parallels)
- Era-based chase (Pre-War, Vintage, Modern)
- Attribute chase (e.g., portraits, tape patterns)

### D. Rarity Quests
Chase specific releases driven by key hits or product innovations.
- Case chase (collect a season of case hits)
- Nameplate (complete name / jersey)
- Jigsaw (complete the patch)
- Autograph chase (e.g., only 1/1 autos)
- Relic chase (e.g., Gold Logoman)

### E. Program Quests *(Product / Campaign Driven)*
Chase specific releases driven by product innovation.
- Three Kings (collect RDPA, Bowman 1st Super Auto, Chrome Rookie Super Auto)
- The Grail (find all 9 grail cards for a prize)
- MVP Buyback program (Chrome)
- Red Rookie Redemption (Bowman)
- FanCash card (redemption)

### F. Action Quests *(Future State)*
Challenge-oriented quests that reward participation, engagement & collecting behaviors.
- Forging
- Rip streak (purchase on last 3 launch days)
- Check-in/Events (check in at Hobby Shops, Rip Night, Fanatics Fest, National, etc.)
- Nine-up (pre-defined 9-card collecting quest to fill one binder page — bronze to gold levels)
- Fox 'Game of the Week' Chase

## 4. Glossary — Key Quest Naming Conventions

> Quests are not Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive.
> Additional naming options considered: Straight, Flush, Journey.

### Stack
**Definition:** Focused same subject across different types/attributes.

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | 'Flagg' Base Stack — All Cooper Flagg base rookies across products |
| 2 | 'Ohtani' Auto Stack — Ohtani auto from all Chrome cards in 2025 (Chrome, Finest, Chrome Black, etc.) |
| 3/4 | 'Ohtani' SSP Stack — Collecting all the Ohtani mega hit inserts in 2025 (Anime, Helix, Alter Egos, Headliners, Aura, etc.) |

### Set
**Definition:** Focused same attribute across different subjects (same card type across card subjects).

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Insert Set — Collect all Future Stars base inserts in 2025 Chrome BK |
| 2 | Golden Mirror Set — Collect full Golden Mirror set from 2025 Series 1 |
| 3/4 | Anime Set — Collect all 2025 Anime cards from Bowman BB releases |

### Rainbow
**Definition:** Focused collection based on parallels of a subject from a single release.

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | 'Flagg' 2025 Chrome Rainbow (partial) — '25 Chrome, Gold /50 and up |
| 2 | 'Flagg' Chrome Rainbow — '25 Chrome, Super 1/1 through Refractor |
| 3/4 | 'Flagg' Topps Rainbow — '25 Flagship, Chrome, & Finest rainbow |

### Pursuit
**Definition:** Focused same attribute across different subjects.

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Rookie Pursuit — Collect every NBA RC base card from 2025 season |
| 2 | Award Winner Pursuit — All MLB MVPs, Cy Young, & ROY in Gold /50 |
| 3/4 | RDPA Pursuit — Collect 1/1 auto relics of top rookie's debut |

### Run *(Future State)*
**Definition:** Same card type and subject across multiple years (e.g., career run).

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Gold Ohtani Run — Collect a gold /50 Topps Chrome Ohtani every year |
| 2 | Ohtani Golden Mirror Run — Collect an Ohtani Golden Mirror card from every Series 1 release |
| 3/4 | Ohtani Anime Run — Collect an Ohtani Anime card from every calendar year |

## 5. Pyramid of Quests — Tiering & Incentivization

### Quest Distribution

| Level | % of Quests | Reward Type | Objective |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Level 1** | 80% | Digital Badging | Drive digital onboarding, collector engagement and support collecting behaviors |
| **Level 2** | 15% | FanCash / Physical Badge | Incentivize sustained engagement and reinvestment |
| **Level 3** | 4% | Exclusive Cards | Reward complex achievements with tangible scarce assets |
| **Level 4** | 1% | 1/1 Cards & Experience | Create aspirational VIP moments for the rarest accomplishments |

### Incentive Structure

| Level | Incentives |
|-------|------------|
| Level 1 | Digital badging |
| Level 2 | Digital badging + FanCash or physical badges |
| Level 3 | Digital badging, FanCash, or Physical badge + Exclusive Card |
| Level 4 | Digital badging, FanCash, or Physical badge, Exclusive Card + VIP experience |

## 6. Reward Strategy by Level

> Not every quest is equal. Reward tiers should scale with difficulty, scarcity, and commitment — ensuring exclusive cards and experiences retain prestige and long-term collector value.

### Level 1 — Digital Badges on Collect App
**Objective:** Drive digital onboarding, collector engagement, and support collecting behaviors.
- **Quest Types:** Sticker Set, Base Set, Rookie/Prospect Chase, Bronze Nine-Up
- **Why:** High-volume, repeatable & accessible quests. Inclusive of lifelong quests. Rewards reinforce collector identity ("Set Builder", "Prospector").

### Level 2 — FanCash at Milestones & Physical Badge
**Objective:** Incentivize sustained engagement and reinvestment.
- **Quest Types:** Rainbow, Master Set, PSA Set Registry, Silver Nine-Up
- **Why:** Requires meaningful time & spend. Cash rewards encourage reinvestment without diluting card scarcity. Physical badge sent for higher-end quests.

### Level 3 — Exclusive Low-Numbered Auto
**Objective:** Reward complex achievements with tangible scarcity.
- **Quest Types:** Nameplate, Anime Set, Rainbow (multi-product), Gold Nine-Up
- **Why:** Premier collector for Topps. Exclusive cards must be clearly limited and equal to quest difficulty to preserve value.

### Level 4 — 1/1 Card & VIP Experience
**Objective:** Create aspirational VIP moments for the rarest accomplishments.
- **Quest Types:** The Grail, Three Kings, Case Chase, 1-of-1 Super Set (all subjects of a set)
- **Why:** These feats should be rare. Experiential rewards reinforce status & deepen long-term brand loyalty.

## 7. Foundational Quests for MVP

### MVP Quests

| Name | Level | Quest Description | Example Reward |
|------|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| Complete / Parallel Set | 1 | Collect a copy of the entire base set or a specific parallel of a product | Digital badge upon full set completion |
| Insert Set | 1 | Complete a copy of the entire insert set of a product (e.g., Instinct from Topps Chrome Basketball) | Digital badge upon full set completion |
| Chase the Rainbow | 2 | Collect as many parallels from a star player / rookie from a flagship Topps product | Digital badge at each set completion |
| All Star Chase | 2 | Collect full set of all-stars from each retail product's parallel exclusive (e.g., Base, Refractor, Pulsar, XFractor, Red White & Blue) | Special all star team set (shipped) + FanCash at milestones |
| SSP Stack | 3 | Collect "quest-eligible" SSP cards of a player across multiple Topps & Bowman brands (e.g., Ohtani SSP Stack — Aura, Anime, Hidden Gems, etc.) | Physical badge at completion (shipped) + FanCash at milestones + Limited exclusive card at completion |

### Future Quests to Add

1. **Nine-Up** — A pre-defined 9-card collecting journey designed to fill one 'digital' binder page, with each pocket representing a specific card to chase and complete (e.g., 9 on-card Autos of Ohtani in 2025 season)
2. **Award Winner Pursuit** — Collect all MLB MVPs, Cy Young, & ROY in Gold /50
3. **Nationality Run** — Collect all base cards from a specific nationality for a product (e.g., all Japan players from Series 1)
4. **Program Quests** — Bring all ongoing and future program quests (e.g., Three Kings, The Grail) onto Fanatics Collect

## 8. Key Innovations Needed to Drive Quests

Building the operating system for collector quests.

### Collection Engine

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Unified Digital Collection** | Ability to scan / import cards into my Collection in Collect App |
| **Quest Hub & Checklists** | Menu page to see available quests and associated checklists |
| **Quest Progress Tracker** | Quest page to track progress — identifies cards currently owned and cards that are still missing |
| **Marketplace Integration** | Collectors can see / be notified when cards missing from their active quests are listed on Collect |

### Reward System

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Tiered Badge Reward System** | Milestone badges awarded to collectors for scanning cards as part of their quest |
| **FanCash Integration** | FanCash distributed to collectors who hit quest milestones with cards in the Vault |
| **Physical Reward Distribution** | Back-end system to distribute exclusive cards / experiences for collectors who complete Tier 3 / Tier 4 quests |

### Community

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Trade Layer** | Option to opt in to match collectors who have duplicates of each person's missing cards |
| **Quest Leaderboard** | Sub-page leaderboard of collectors who have completed the quests / leading the chase |
| **Personalization** | Quests recommended to collectors based on current cards in their vault / what quests their friends are pursuing |

## Key Differences from v5 (Refined Version)

For reference, notable changes between this v3 draft and the refined v5:

- **Goal statement:** v3 says "inspire new collecting journeys" — v5 shifts to "build a structured ecosystem that increases appetite, depth, and lifetime engagement"
- **Action Quests expanded in v3:** Includes Forging, Rip streak, Fox 'Game of the Week' Chase, and Nine-Up at this level — v5 simplifies to check-ins and streaks only
- **Reward type language tightened in v5:** e.g., Level 3 moves from "Exclusive Cards" to "Recognition & Scarcity Asset"; Level 4 from "1/1 Cards & Experience" to "Prestige & VIP Experience"
- **Level 3 quests:** v3 includes "Gold Nine-Up" and "Rainbow (multi-product)"; v5 replaces with "Double Rainbow (multi-product)" and drops Nine-Up
- **Level 4 quests:** v3 includes "Case Chase"; v5 drops it
- **All Star quest renamed:** "All Star Chase" in v3 becomes "All-Star Pursuit" in v5
- **Collection Engine features:** v3 has "Quest Hub & Checklists" + "Quest Progress Tracker"; v5 consolidates into "Quest Hub" and adds "Guided Onboarding"
- **GTM Strategy added in v5:** Phased rollout (Pilot → Expand → Scale) not present in v3
- **Set naming:** v3 omits "Parallel Set" example under the Set glossary entry; v5 adds it
