# Goals and priorities

## 1. Collector quests (strategic bet — next quarter)
Build a quest and challenge engine that supports collecting habits and lets collectors organize collections the way they want to collect — whether that's chasing a rainbow, collecting specific players, or completing a set. Lean into the motivations that drive collectors: completionism, status, and social sharing.

## 2. Team development
Ensure the product design team has clear goals with consistent check-ins. Build a clear progression program so everyone knows what they need to do to get to the next level.

## 3. Enable building blocks for Topps programmes
A specific initiative within Collector Quests — build the foundational building blocks that enable Topps programmes (starting with FanCash Trading Cards for 2026 Flagship Football) to plug into the quest and challenge engine. FanCash Trading Cards bridge collecting and fantasy sports, letting collectors earn FanCash based on real player performance across tiered cards (Silver, Gold, Black, Platinum).

## 4. Design system adoption and AI-readiness
Ensure our design system is aligned between Figma and code, owned by the design team end-to-end. Use MCP tooling (Figma Console MCP, FigmaLint, Story UI) to establish a one-directional Figma → code pipeline with validation and linting. Evaluating collaboration with Southleft to audit, set up tooling, and train the team.

## 5. Collector innovation (ongoing)
Continuously think about the next bets — not just next quarter but the quarter after. Understand collector needs from their experience, not just meeting them but exceeding them and making everything feel magical.
