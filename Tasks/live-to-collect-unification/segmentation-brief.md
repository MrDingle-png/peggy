# User Segmentation Brief: Live → Collect Unification

**Requested by:** Michael Dingle, VP Product Design
**Brief for:** Data / Analytics team
**Priority:** High
**Required by:** [DATE — to be confirmed]

## Why we need this

We are planning the migration of Fanatics Live users to Fanatics Collect as part of the Live → Collect Unification project. Before we can plan user communications, lifecycle flows, paid marketing wind-down, or creator briefings, we need to understand who we are actually moving.

This brief asks for a segmentation of our user base to identify the size, value, and behaviour profile of each cohort — particularly the Live-only users who have never opened Collect.

This analysis will directly inform: migration sequencing, re-engagement campaign design, paid attribution planning, creator outreach prioritisation, and exec reporting.

---

## Segments to define

Please define and size the following four segments. The account system is shared between Live and Collect, so we expect user-level linking to be possible — please confirm this below.

### Segment A — Live-only (priority)
Users who have the Live app installed and have **never opened Collect**.

Break this segment down by recency:
- **Active** — opened Live in the last 30 days
- **Lapsed** — opened Live 31–90 days ago
- **Dormant** — not opened Live in 90+ days

### Segment B — Cross-app users
Users who have opened both Live and Collect.

Break this segment down by primary app:
- **Live-primary** — more sessions in Live than Collect in last 90 days
- **Collect-primary** — more sessions in Collect than Live in last 90 days
- **Balanced** — roughly equal usage across both

### Segment C — Collect-only
Users who have opened Collect but have never opened Live. These users are unaffected by the migration but useful as a baseline for comparison.

### Segment D — Lapsed / unengaged (both apps)
Users who have installed one or both apps but have not opened either in 90+ days. Flag the size of this group — they are a separate re-engagement question.

## Special cohorts — please flag separately

Within Segment A (Live-only), we need three additional cuts:

**1. Active sellers**
Users in Segment A who have completed at least one sale on Live in the last 90 days. These users are the highest-risk cohort — selling parity in Collect is not yet complete. We need to know their size and GMV contribution before planning any migration communications.

**2. Power buyers**
Users in Segment A whose total GMV over the last 12 months exceeds [THRESHOLD — please suggest based on distribution, or provide P90 GMV as a reference]. These are high-value users who warrant direct outreach rather than bulk communications.

**3. High-engagement Live users with zero Collect opens**
Users in Segment A (Live-only) who are in the top quartile for Live engagement (sessions per week or GMV) but have never opened Collect. This is the "true believer" cohort — users for whom Live is their primary experience of Fanatics. Understanding their size and profile is important for narrative and migration design.

---

## Questions for the data team to confirm before starting

1. **Account linking** — Can Live and Collect users be linked at the account level using the shared account system? If not, what is the best available proxy (email, device ID, etc.)?

2. **First open data** — How far back does reliable "first open" data go for both apps? Is there any gap in historical coverage we should be aware of?

3. **Push and email opt-in** — Do we have push notification and email opt-in status at the individual user level for both apps? Are these stored consistently, or are there differences in how opt-in is tracked across Live vs. Collect?

4. **Session data reliability** — Is there any known gap or inconsistency in session tracking across the two apps that would affect engagement comparisons?

---

## Output format requested

**Primary deliverable:** A summary table showing segment sizes, % of total base, and key metrics (GMV, engagement, opt-in rates) for each segment.

**Secondary deliverable:** A brief narrative summary (half page) of the key findings — particularly any surprises in the Live-only cohort size, value, or behaviour that should inform migration planning.

**Format:** Shareable Google Sheet or Notion page, plus a short written summary.

**Audience:** This will be used by Product, Product Marketing, Growth/Lifecycle, and Paid Marketing leads. It will also be referenced in an exec presentation on the unification plan.
